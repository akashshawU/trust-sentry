# AI Trust Agent

A comprehensive framework for building, evaluating, and maintaining trustworthy AI systems across the full lifecycle of model development and deployment.

## Overview

The AI Trust Agent provides a structured, multi-dimensional approach to AI governance. It continuously monitors and scores AI systems across eight foundational pillars of trust, enabling organizations to identify risks, enforce policies, and demonstrate accountability to stakeholders and regulators.

---

## The 8 Pillars of AI Trust

### 1. Trust Score
A unified, real-time composite score that aggregates signals across all eight pillars into a single actionable metric. The trust score gives teams and leadership an at-a-glance view of overall AI system health and flags degradation before it becomes a risk.

### 2. Privacy
Ensures that AI systems handle personal and sensitive data in accordance with user expectations and legal requirements. This pillar covers data minimization, anonymization, consent tracking, and protection against inference attacks that could expose private information.

### 3. Access Control
Governs who and what can interact with AI models, data pipelines, and system outputs. This pillar enforces role-based and attribute-based access policies, audits authentication events, and prevents unauthorized use or manipulation of AI assets.

### 4. Fairness
Detects and mitigates bias in model inputs, outputs, and decisions. This pillar evaluates performance parity across demographic groups, monitors for disparate impact, and provides tooling to audit training data and model predictions for discriminatory patterns.

### 5. Explainability
Makes AI decision-making interpretable to developers, auditors, and end users. This pillar surfaces feature importance, generates human-readable rationales for model outputs, and supports audit trails that document how and why a given decision was reached.

### 6. Security
Protects AI systems against adversarial attacks, data poisoning, model theft, and prompt injection. This pillar implements threat modeling specific to machine learning systems, monitors for anomalous behavior, and enforces hardening standards across the AI supply chain.

### 7. Regulatory Compliance
Tracks adherence to applicable laws, standards, and internal policies — including GDPR, CCPA, the EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, and sector-specific regulations. This pillar generates compliance reports, maps controls to regulatory requirements, and alerts teams to gaps or upcoming obligations.

### 8. Operational Resilience
Ensures AI systems remain reliable, available, and performant under real-world conditions. This pillar monitors model drift, data quality degradation, infrastructure health, and failover readiness — so that trust is maintained not just at launch, but continuously in production.
